Why even create scheduling sheets?

With an understaffed department things can get tricky.

The Computer Science Department at the University of Delaware is home to some of the most dedicated and hardworking professors. Nonetheless, the professors and department staff will be the first to tell you that we are significantly understaffed. As a result of this, most undergraduate students have a professors as their official degree advisor. This leads for CS students to be significantly underadvised compared to departments who have teams dedicated to overseeing student advising.

 

To help myself stay organized, I used a small personal scheduling sheet my first year. Over time, a few of my friends asked me to make similar Google sheets for them. After making about two or three, I got frustrated with making individual sheets and wondered why we never had any standard Google Sheets as a department.

 

While in a CPU meeting I remembered how frustrated I felt at answering the same questions, so I pitched the idea of having Google Sheets for all of the programs (majors and concentrations) in the Computer Science department. Professor Yarrington and the other CPUs were enthusiastic and encouraged me to start this small side project. Currently, I have made a sheet for every concentration and major within the department. These sheets should be finished (based off of the UD CIS catalog). I also created a README document that details how to best use the sheets and frequently asked questions. Professor Yarrington and the other CPUs were a great help in this process!
