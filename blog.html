My Writing

Click the Blue Titles to download a Microsoft Word file of my writing.

  • Mission Statement Deploying ethos, pathos, and logos I crafted an effective mission statement aimed to describe HenHacks hackathon, a hackathon I currently work for. Being the largest hackathon in Delaware provides a draw as this is the hackathon with the most opportunities nearby for students. I detailed why HenHacks is an important emblem of the Delaware computer science community.

  • Short Report Responding to college students’ lack of insurance knowledge I empower Liberty Mutual to fill this need by creating a customer-insurance connection program. Many of my coworkers and myself were unaware of the breadth of insurance plans that we may need. To make insurance more accessible and easy for customers to get insurance plans they may need, a quiz-matching sign up is approved.

  • Corporate Memo To better understand how to effectively communicate professionally in difficult scenarios, I was tasked with navigating an accidental tweet made by DiGiorno's pizza regarding domestic violence. Acting as a higher up who had to come up with a business plan as to how to address our internal employees and subsequently how to rebuild our business’ image. I pitched a full apology and anthology of next steps to show DiGiorno’s commitments to fixing their mistake.

  • Identifying Persuasive Rhetoric Grief is a powerful feeling that Jesmyn Ward skillfully explores through her essay ‘On Witness and Respair’. Diving into this essay we break down her ethos, logos, and pathos’ impact on the reader and their understanding of her perspective. I am now better able to understand writing strategies and author’s intentions.

  • Measuring Undergraduate Understanding in Computer Science In my EDUC485: Multimedia Literacy course, we were tasked with creating a research paper catered to our educational technology interests. As a long time Introduction to Computer Science teaching assistant and someone interested in computer science education, I wanted to better understand what forms of assessment have the most positive impact on students. I found that project-based assessments are typically more successful than exams as they provide more room for developer growth and incentivize student interests.
